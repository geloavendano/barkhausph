Getting started — the basics

The five things every other chapter builds on: logging in, the branch switcher, the layout, the Calendar, and the booking drawer.

0.1 Logging in

The admin portal lives at barkhaus.ph/admin/. There is no password — you sign in with your Google account 1. Access is an allow-list: only Google accounts registered as admins can get in (see Chapter 10 to request access).

📷01-login-gate.pngDesktop 1280
Current UI: the logged-out gate — Barkhaus logo, “Admin Portal”, and the “Sign in with Google” button.
  • 1 Sign in with Google button
If you sign in and land back on the sign-in screen, your Google account isn't on the admin list yet — see Chapter 10.

0.2 The branch switcher — the most important control

The top bar has Estancia / Eastwood tabs 1. Every page shows only the selected branch's data. Switching jumps the Calendar back to today on the "All" tab and reloads bookings, the check-in queue, members, inventory and reports for that branch. If something you expect is "missing", the selected branch is the first thing to check.

0.3 Layout & navigation

Desktop: a left sidebar lists Calendar · Bookings · Pending · Members · Inventory · Reports · Admin Guide; the top bar holds the branch tabs, your greeting, and Sign out (↪) — always sign out on shared devices.

Mobile: the sidebar becomes a bottom bar — Calendar · Bookings · Pending · More 1. Tap More for Members, Inventory, Reports and this guide 2. The yellow ＋ button (FAB) floats above the bar 3 — that's where you create bookings, walk-ins, and blocks.

0.4 The Calendar — your home screen

The Calendar opens on today, All services. A view switch in the date bar offers four ways to look at the same bookings — on mobile it's a dropdown:

  • Day — a 9 AM–10 PM timeline; each card is a booking, each hatched stripe is blocked time.
  • Week — seven day-columns; multi-day hotel stays draw as one bar across the days, with timed grooming/daycare/studio listed underneath.
  • Month — a month grid with event bars; tap +N more to see a day's full list, or tap a day to open it.
  • List — a simple agenda of the month's bookings grouped by day. Tap Today to jump to today.

Reading a Day card: pet name with 🐶/🐱, the room/package, and owner when there's room. The card's color is the assigned room or groomer's color (set in Inventory). A small status dot shows the booking status (Appendix A), a ★ means a member discount, and a red "now" line marks the current time on today. The service tabs (All · Hotel · Grooming · Daycare · Studio) filter by service and show each day's count.

  • 1 Day / Week / Month / List switch

On desktop, the left sidebar lists every room/groomer with its color and count — click one to filter the calendar to it. On mobile, use the ⊟ filter button in the date bar. The list narrows to the relevant resources when a service tab is active.
For a historical or hard-to-find record, use Bookings. In addition to the service tabs, it can filter by booking status, payment status, and source (online/admin/walk-in), and search by reference, owner name/email, or pet name.

0.5 The booking drawer — everything about one booking

Click any booking — on the Calendar, Bookings, Pending, or Reports — and the same drawer opens. Top to bottom: reference, booked time, source / creator, Print, and ✏ Edit booking; the pet header; status & payment pills; a red medical-notes box (read it before handling the pet); temperament, vaccines, waivers, and any uploaded vaccine or grooming-reference files; service details with the Assign button for room/groomer; owner & emergency/vet; Bill, Payments (+ Record), admin/check-in notes, and Change history. A pinned sticky footer holds the booking-status and payment-status dropdowns plus Update. Changing status anywhere in the app happens in that footer.

0.6 The app keeps itself fresh

You rarely need to reload. The Calendar and Bookings update instantly on database changes (including a customer booking online), use a five-minute fallback refresh, and refresh whenever you return to the tab. Press R to refresh on demand; Esc closes whatever panel or drawer is open (full list in Appendix C).

Ch 1

Managing the shift schedule

Before guests can be booked into a groomer, that groomer needs service hours on the calendar. This is usually a manager task, done a few days ahead.

1.1 Updating groomer service schedule via Inventory

A grooming time-slot only appears to customers (and in Add Booking) when at least one groomer is scheduled for that day and the slot is still free after blocks and existing bookings. You set those hours in Inventory.

  1. Open Inventory (📦) → Groomers tab 1.
  2. Tap the groomer — a month calendar opens on today 2.
  3. Pick a date and set the start & end service hours and the last service time (the latest a new groom can begin that day) 3.
  4. To schedule many days at once, use Add in bulk — it overwrites the hours across all the dates you pick.
📷37-inventory.pngDesktop 1280
Current UI: Inventory → Groomers with a live month schedule, saved hours, last-service time, Add in bulk, and resource details.
  • 1 Groomers tab
  • 2 Month schedule on today
  • 3 Hours + last service time
The dashboard shows an amber "Grooming availability needs attention" banner whenever any of the next 14 days has no groomer hours. Use Fix in Inventory on the banner to fill them — otherwise customers can't book grooming on those days.

1.2 Time off & blocks (lunch, leave, repairs)

For a one-off absence — a groomer's lunch, a half-day, a cage under repair — use a block instead of editing hours. Blocks remove time from the public site and the admin wizard at the same time.

  1. ＋ FAB → 🚫 Block Schedule.
  2. Choose the resource type 1 (Hotel Room / Groomer / Studio) and which one 2.
  3. Tap one or more dates on the mini calendar 3 (a count confirms how many).
  4. Set start/end time 4, write a short reason ("Lunch", "On leave"), and save.

Blocks appear on the Calendar as hatched stripes in the resource's color, sitting beside (never under) any booking in the same hour. Tap one to view or delete it — removing a block frees the time everywhere immediately. For a long absence (weeks), prefer marking the groomer/room unavailable in Inventory instead.

Starting your shift

First ten minutes at the counter: see who's coming today, and make sure every grooming booking has a groomer.

2.1 Checking today's bookings

Two quick reads:

  • Calendar → Today, All — the timeline shows the shape of the day (who's in which room/groomer, overlaps, blocked time). Switch to List view for a plain top-to-bottom agenda if you prefer a checklist.
  • Pending (🐾) — the front-desk queue: who's arriving, who's here, and who's leaving (Chapter 3).

2.2 Assigning unassigned groomers

Grooming bookings where the customer chose "Any available" arrive with no specific groomer. They must be assigned before the guest is here — the app blocks check-in until a groomer (or room, for hotel) is set. The app makes these easy to spot:

  • 1 An amber banner — "Grooming bookings need a groomer" — counts how many are unassigned today and tomorrow, with an Assign in Calendar button.
  • 2 On the Calendar, unassigned cards carry an amber left edge and a ⚠ marker (a ⚠ Assign badge in List view).

To assign: open the booking → in the Groomer section tap Assign 3 → pick a groomer → save. The card immediately recolors to that groomer. (Same flow assigns a Room for an unassigned hotel booking.)

Ch 3

Checking a guest in

A guest arrives. Find them, settle any payment, note their condition, and mark them checked in.

Find the booking→Confirm payment→Check-in notes→Status: Checked in

3.1 Finding the booking

The Pending (🐾) page groups today's activity into sections:

  • 1 Due for Check-in — red, with a ⚠ Overdue flag. These are bookings still marked Confirmed whose arrival time has already passed. Clear these first.
  • 2 Awaiting Check-In — today's appointments and hotel arrivals that aren't overdue yet.
  • 3 In Progress — guests already checked in.
  • 4 Due for Checkout — hotel guests leaving today; anything past its date shows ⚠ Overdue (Chapter 4).

Each card shows service, reference, pet, owner and time. The search box here searches all bookings (not just today's) by reference, owner name/email, or pet name — handy when a guest arrives early or off-schedule.

Current UI: Pending page with customer rows collapsed, showing Pending Confirmation, Due for Check-in, Awaiting Check-In, In Progress, and Due for Checkout counts.
  • 1 Due for Check-in (red, overdue)
  • 2 Awaiting Check-In

  • 4 Due for Checkout

3.2 Confirming payment

Open the card and check the payment pill. Successfully paid online bookings arrive Paid; when a manual-transfer receipt exists, it appears under Payments. For walk-ins, phone bookings, or balances, collect now and record it: Payments → + Record → amount, type, method, and reference — the signed-in admin is recorded automatically — then set the payment status in the sticky footer to match (partially paid / paid).

3.3 Adding check-in notes

In the drawer, Check-in notes (+ Add / Edit) opens a short condition report that adapts to the service:

  • Grooming — remarks + five Yes/No checks: vaccination complete, skin & coat, ears/nose/eyes, nails, joints.
  • Hotel — physical inspection notes, personal items left with the pet, add-on purchases.
  • Daycare — remarks + add-on purchases.
Fill this in with the owner present. A pre-existing skin condition noted at check-in protects both the pet and the staff later.

3.4 Updating status to Checked in

Before you flip the status, confirm: a room/groomer is assigned (the app blocks check-in otherwise), medical notes read, vaccines/waivers checked (especially play-park consent). Then in the sticky footer: set booking status to checked in → Update. The guest moves to In Progress.

Ch 4

Completing the booking / checking out

Service done, guest leaving: settle the final bill and close the booking.

Find the booking→Settle extra charges→Status: Completed

4.1 Finding the booking

Open the guest from Due for Checkout (hotel) or In Progress (grooming / daycare / studio) on the Pending page. If they're not where you expect, use the search box.

4.2 Updating status to Completed

  1. Check for extra charges the booking didn't include up front:
    • Hotel late pick-up — every hour past 2 PM adds the hourly late fee; more than 6 hours past counts as an extra night; not picked up by 8 PM = an additional night.
    • Daycare extra hours — hours beyond the first 3 (or beyond what was prepaid) at the per-size hourly rate.
    • Any unpaid in-store add-ons.
  2. Collect the balance and record it (Payments → + Record); set payment status to paid.
  3. Set booking status to completed → Update. The guest leaves the Pending page; the slot/room is free.

Ch 5

Accepting walk-in visitors

A customer arrives without a booking. Let them book themselves in on your device — fastest, and it captures their own waivers and details.

Tap the ＋ FAB → 🚶 Walk-in. This opens the customer-facing booking page in a new tab, secured by a one-time pass that only the admin app can create 1. Hand the customer the tablet/phone; they fill in pet, waivers and contact details themselves. Differences from an online booking: no convenience fee, no online payment — the final screen shows the amount to collect in-store 2 — and the booking is tagged Walk-in.

It appears on the Calendar and Pending page instantly. Then: assign a room/groomer if needed (Chapter 2.2), collect payment and record it (Chapter 3.2), and check them in (Chapter 3.4).

Ch 6

Confirming inquiries

Someone calls or messages to book. Create the booking for them, and verify any membership they claim.

6.1 Creating a confirmed booking

Tap ＋ FAB → 📋 Add Booking. The wizard walks six steps:

Service→Schedule→Pet→Owner→Details→Summary
  • Service — Grooming, Pet Hotel, Daycare, or Studio.
  • Schedule — service-specific (size/package/add-ons/groomer/slot for grooming; dates/room/times for hotel; date/times for daycare). Prices follow Appendix B and update live.
  • Owner — search by phone or email first 1; tap an existing match to pre-fill, so one customer stays one record 2.
  • Details — hotel feeding/meds/emergency/vet; the waiver toggles (confirm verbally); internal notes.
  • Summary — apply a member code if any (Apply → ✓ and the discount appears); set status (usually Confirmed) and payment; put your name under Recorded by; Create booking.
If the owner's email is filled in, a confirmation email is sent immediately on create — admin bookings don't wait for payment.
📷26-owner-search.pngDesktop 1280
Capture: Owner step with the search dropdown showing an existing match.
  • 1 Search field
  • 2 Matching owner row

6.2 Looking up membership ID records

When a customer claims a membership, verify it on Members: enter the code and pet name and read the result — ✓ valid (with tier, coverage and expiry), pet name doesn't match, expired (with the date), or not found / inactive.

  • Standard — valid at the home branch only.
  • Passport coverage — a member with no home branch is valid at both branches.
  • Membership type is separate from coverage: Standard or Renewal, which determines the applicable discount rate.

You can also just type the code on the booking Summary step (§6.1) and press Apply — a wrong-branch, expired, or inactive code is rejected with the reason.

Ch 7

Uploading & updating members

Membership records are kept as a spreadsheet and imported on the Members page. The same import both adds new members and updates existing ones.

7.1 New member upload

On Members, use the CSV upload card. The file needs exactly these headers:

Membership ID · Pet Name · Breed · Valid Until Date · Branch · Membership Type

Membership Type is required and must be Standard or Renewal. Leave Branch blank only for Passport coverage; otherwise use a recognized Barkhaus branch name.

  1. Choose the file, then review the filename and row count before importing.
  2. Proceed — progress shows while it runs.
  3. When it finishes, the dashboard downloads a row-level import report noting each row's status and next action. Open it to confirm everything imported cleanly.
📷36-members.pngDesktop 1280
Capture: Members page — the CSV upload card with a chosen file, row count, and proceed/cancel.
  • 1 CSV upload card
  • 2 Filename + row-count review

7.2 Membership ID updates

To change an existing membership — a new Valid Until Date (renewal), a corrected pet name, a branch change — edit that row in the spreadsheet and re-upload. The import matches on Membership ID: rows whose ID already exists are updated, new IDs are added. Check the downloaded report to confirm each row landed as "updated" or "added" as expected.

Keep the master spreadsheet as the single source of truth and always re-upload the whole sheet — that way the import report is a complete record of the latest state.

Ch 8

Closing your shift

Leave the board clean for the next shift: nothing should be sitting in the wrong status.

8.1 Updating bookings supposed to be checked in / completed

Open Pending (🐾) and sweep the overdue groups top to bottom:

  • 1 Due for Check-in (red) — guests still Confirmed past their arrival time. For each: if they came, check them in; if they truly no-showed, follow your branch policy (call the owner, or cancel in the sticky footer so the slot frees).
  • 2 In Progress — anyone who has actually left should be moved to completed (settle any balance first, Chapter 4).
  • 3 Due for Checkout — hotel guests flagged ⚠ Overdue: collect the late/extra charges and complete, or extend the stay (Edit booking) if they're staying another night.

When all three are clear, the next shift opens to a queue that reflects reality.

Ch 9

Managing incentives — reports

The Reports page totals a groomer's completed grooming work over a date range — the basis for grooming incentives.

9.1 Generating a groomer report

  1. Open Reports (📊) and confirm the right branch is selected up top.
  2. Set From and To dates 1 (e.g. a pay period).
  3. Pick the Groomer 2.

The summary tiles 3 update for that groomer and range:

  • Subtotal sales — total value of their completed grooming bookings.
  • Grooming services — the service portion (excludes add-ons).
  • Add-ons — total of all add-ons sold.
  • Demat / deshed add-ons — the de-matting / de-shedding portion, called out separately because it's commission-relevant.

Below the tiles is the list of completed grooming bookings that make up the totals 4; a row tagged Demat / Deshed included one of those add-ons. Tap any row to open its drawer and verify the detail. Reports are read on screen — use the date range to scope a period, and capture/print the page if you need a copy.

Use Reports → Payment Status Check when a customer says Maya completed payment but the booking still looks unpaid or pending. Enter the Barkhaus reference (for example BH-597A57) and select Check Payment Status. Ch 10

Requesting additional admin access

Admin access is an allow-list of Google accounts. New staff, or a change to which branch you can see, is granted by the owner/manager.

10.1 If you're a staff member

To get access — or to change which branch(es) you can see — send the owner/manager:

  • the exact Google email you'll sign in with (the address must match exactly);
  • your name (shown in the greeting and used on audit trails);
  • which branch(es) you need — Estancia, Eastwood, or both.

Once they confirm it's set, go to barkhaus.ph/admin/ and sign in with that Google account. If you still land on the sign-in screen, the email on the list doesn't match the one you used — double-check it with the manager.

Access is tied to a specific Google address. A different or mistyped email — even one character off — won't get in. Sign out of other Google accounts first if your device has several.

10.2 Manager note — how access is granted

Admins live in the admin_users table, each with optional branch scope:

  • Both branches — leave the branch scope empty (NULL).
  • One branch — set the branch scope to just that branch; that admin then only sees and acts on that branch's data (member look-ups still work across branches).

Adding or changing an admin is a database change run by the owner/manager (it's not self-service in the app). Keep a record of who has access and to which branch, and remove access promptly when someone leaves.
