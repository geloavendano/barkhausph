Platform overview & actors

Barkhaus is a two-branch pet-services business (Estancia and Eastwood, Metro Manila) offering grooming, pet hotel, daycare, and self-shoot studio services. The platform consists of a public marketing site, a customer self-service booking system with online payment, and an internal admin dashboard for branch operations — all served from a single domain, barkhaus.ph.

Actors

ActorCapabilities
VisitorBrowses landing page, services, locations, membership info, blog, FAQ. No account needed.
Customer (online)Books a service end-to-end and pays online via card / e-wallet. No account — identified per-booking by contact details; repeat customers are matched by email/phone.
Customer (walk-in)Completes the same booking form on a staff device at the counter, without online payment (pays in store).
Branch staff / AdminSigns in with Google. Full admin dashboard: calendar, bookings, check-in/out, payments, members, inventory, blocked schedules. Access controlled by an allow-list (admin_users).
SystemAutomated behaviors: payment webhooks, confirmation emails, 15-minute pending-booking auto-cancel, live data refresh.

Online booking system

A 7-step wizard at /booking.html. State lives client-side until payment; the booking row is only created when the customer proceeds to pay (or, for walk-ins, on submission).

3.1 Step flow

StepNameContents & gates
1LocationEstancia or Eastwood. Selecting auto-advances. Everything downstream (availability, hours, resources) is scoped to this branch.
2ServiceGrooming / Pet Hotel / Daycare / Studio / Events. Studio is enabled only at branches that have studios (Eastwood). Events shows an info page with a back link (inquiry only, no booking).
3SpecsService-specific: size/package/add-ons (grooming), size/dates/room (hotel), size/date/times (daycare), date/slot (studio). See §3.2–§3.5.
4ScheduleService-specific scheduling: groomer + time slot (grooming), drop-off/pick-up times + play park (hotel). For daycare/studio this is merged into step 3.
5PetPet profile, vaccines, membership. See §3.6.
6Owner ("You")Name, email, mobile, referral source ("How did you hear about us" — selecting Other reveals a free-text field that is stored with the booking).
7Waivers + SummaryService-specific waivers (all required), then a grouped summary (Booking / Schedule / Pet Details / Health & Care / Owner Details) with a hierarchical price breakdown and Confirm.

Every step has explicit validation; the Continue button is visually dimmed when incomplete but still tappable — tapping it fires an alert naming exactly what's missing (deliberate UX choice over a dead button). Back-navigation preserves all entered state.

3.2 Grooming

  • Sizes: small / medium / large / giant dog, cat — sets all prices.
  • Packages: Bath & Dry (30 min), Basic (1 hr), Premium (2 hrs), Ala Carte (no base; requires ≥1 add-on).
  • Add-ons: fixed-price items; Face Trim is size-priced and included free with Premium (shown "included", not toggleable); Dematting / Deshedding are assessment-priced in store (selected but billed later).
  • Duration: Bath & Dry is 30 minutes; Basic is 1 hour; Premium is 2 hours; Ala Carte defaults to 1 hour. Selecting Dematting and/or Deshedding adds a single 30-minute buffer, so Bath & Dry with either/both blocks 60 minutes.
  • Groomer preference: a specific groomer or "Any available" (clearly disclaimed: may be reassigned if absent).
  • Slot availability (hourly slots 9 AM–5 PM) excludes, per groomer: existing bookings on the appointment date (each booking blocks its computed duration), recurring schedule restrictions (days off, daily start/end), and one-off blocked schedules. Any available shows a slot only if at least one groomer survives all three checks after subtracting unassigned ("any") bookings already holding that slot; a specific groomer is hidden when unassigned bookings would overflow onto them as the last free groomer.
Availability is keyed on the appointment date (service_date), never the date the booking was created.

3.3 Pet Hotel

  • Cats only at Eastwood — selecting Cat at Estancia is blocked with a redirect hint.
  • Dates: check-in/check-out (min 1 night, no past dates). Nights counter shown. Stays of 7+ nights display a complimentary Bath & Dry note.
  • Room availability: rooms are filtered by pet-size compatibility (small dog → small/medium/large cage; large/giant → large cage; cats → cabin/villa), per-cage-type capacity, overlapping confirmed stays, and room locks. The checkout date is non-blocking for turnover, so a room checking out on a date is available to another booking checking in that same date. Fully booked rooms render disabled.
  • Pricing is per cage type × day type (weekday vs weekend) — see §9. A live rate breakdown (weekday nights × rate + weekend nights × rate) appears as soon as dates + room are chosen.
  • Drop-off / pick-up times: hour dropdowns limited to branch operating hours for the respective date. Pick-up after 2 PM accrues the late ladder (§9). Values stored as bare hour integers.
  • Play park consent (dogs only): explicit Yes/No; "No" is honored operationally (pet stays in room) and flagged in the admin drawer.
  • Cat villa: selecting a villa reveals an optional second-cat section.
  • Health & care details: feeding instructions, medications, emergency contact, vet clinic/contact/address — all carried to the admin drawer.

3.4 Daycare

  • Size + date + drop-off/pick-up times. Pick-up must be after drop-off (validated).
  • Open pickup toggle hides the pick-up field; pet stays until closing ("last pet out") and extras settle at pickup.
  • Price = base (first 3 hours) + per-size hourly rate for each additional hour, computed live with an hours label.

3.5 Self-shoot Studio

  • Eastwood only. Date + hourly slot (10 AM–9 PM); slots disabled when taken by other bookings or blocked schedules.
  • Session price is settled at the studio (summary shows "Set at check-out"); the Studio Usage Agreement waiver is required.

3.6 Pet profile, vaccines & senior waiver

  • Pet name, animal (locked to dog/cat by the size already chosen), sex, breed, age (+years/months), temperament, medical notes.
  • Senior / pre-existing-condition waiver auto-appears when age ≥ 6 years or medical notes are non-empty (age in months never triggers it). It must be checked to continue, and resurfaces as a required waiver block at step 7.
  • Vaccine declaration: per-vaccine checklist — dogs: Anti-rabies, 5/6/8-in-1, Kennel Cough/Bordetella, Tick & flea; cats: Anti-rabies, All-in-1, Anti-parasitic. The full map (checked and unchecked) is stored so the admin sees ✓/✕ per vaccine.
  • Vaccine record upload: customer may upload photo/PDF records (multiple files, to Supabase Storage). When no file is uploaded, an "I will bring my pet's vaccine records to the venue" checkbox is auto-checked (can be unchecked; one of the two paths is required).
  • Membership: Yes/No. Yes requires a member code + pet name that validate together (§8). A failed validation blocks Continue; the error is deliberately generic ("Member not found.") to avoid leaking which part failed.

3.7 Waivers (step 7)

WaiverApplies to
General terms / service waiverAll services (text varies per service: grooming risks, hotel house rules & abandonment policy, daycare play-park release)
Health / vaccine declarationAll services
Media consent (photos/videos)All services; declining is allowed and flags "do not post this pet" in admin
Senior / medical waiverConditional (§3.6)
Play park consentHotel dogs (Yes/No captured at step 4)
Studio usage agreementStudio only

All applicable waivers must be accepted before Confirm enables. Acceptance states + signing timestamp persist on the booking.

Payments & booking lifecycle

4.1 Checkout (online)

  1. On Confirm, the system first re-checks availability (room still free for the date span / slot still free). If taken in the meantime: toast ("That room was just booked" / "That time slot was just taken") and the customer is returned to the relevant step with a refreshed grid and cleared selection.
  2. When hosted checkout is enabled, a pending booking row is created (status pending, payment unpaid) with a generated reference number (BH-XXXXXX), and the customer is redirected to Maya Checkout. A convenience fee may be added for online payment only. During migration, manual transfer remains active; Maya and the retained PayMongo integration are hidden and disabled.
  3. The pending booking holds the slot/room for ~15 minutes (communicated on the payment loading screen). A scheduled job auto-cancels pending+unpaid bookings older than 15 minutes, freeing inventory.

4.2 Payment success

  • Hosted-payment webhooks confirm payment; processing is idempotent. Maya events are verified server-to-server against Maya's Payments API, including status, reference, currency, and amount. The dormant PayMongo path retains signature verification and duplicate-event handling.
  • The booking flips to confirmed / paid; all detail rows are written (service details with appointment date, add-ons, itemized charges, vaccines, waiver record, uploaded vaccine documents).
  • A confirmation email is sent (§7) and the customer returns to a success screen: full booking recap, price breakdown, "arrive 15 minutes early" note, and a print/PDF prompt.

4.3 Payment cancelled / failed

  • Returning with ?payment=cancelled or failed shows a payment-return screen (not step 1): reference number, full recap, price breakdown, and two actions.
  • Retry Payment — creates a fresh checkout for the same pending booking.
  • Edit booking details — cancels the pending booking server-side (freeing inventory), then restores the entire wizard from a session snapshot at the Summary step; the customer can walk back through fully pre-filled steps, change anything, and confirm again (new reference number).
  • If the snapshot is missing (e.g. new browser), degrade gracefully to step 1 with a toast.

4.4 Booking statuses

StatusMeaning / transitions
pendingAwaiting payment. → confirmed (webhook) · → cancelled (auto-cancel, customer edit, admin)
confirmedPaid online, or created by admin. → checked_in · → cancelled / rejected
checked_inPet on premises (requires assigned room/groomer). → completed
completedService done, settled. Terminal.
cancelled / rejectedSlot freed; kept in history (greyed + strikethrough). Terminal.

Payment statuses: unpaid → partially_paid → paid, plus refunded. Admin sets these manually for in-store money; online payments set paid automatically.

Walk-in mode

The same booking page, opened from the admin FAB in walk-in mode so a counter customer fills their own details on a staff device.

  • Access control: the admin app mints a single-use token and opens booking.html?walkin=1&token=…. The page verifies and consumes the token before unlocking; without a valid token an Access Restricted screen is shown. This prevents customers from self-serving the fee-free flow from home.
  • Differences from online: convenience fee is ₱0; no payment gateway — the final screen shows the amount to collect in-store and a printable recap with full booking details; booking is created immediately as confirmed/unpaid with source walkin.
  • The booking appears on the admin calendar in real time; staff collect payment and record it in the drawer.

Admin dashboard

React single-page app at /admin/. Full operational walkthrough with screenshots lives in docs/admin-guide.html; this section specifies the functionality.

6.1 Authentication (SSO) & shell

  • Google sign-in only (OAuth via Supabase Auth). After sign-in, the user's email is checked against the admin_users allow-list; non-admins stay at the gate.
  • Top bar: branch switcher (all data branch-scoped), greeting, sign-out. Desktop sidebar with Calendar, Bookings, Check In, Members, Inventory, Reports, and an Admin Guide link; mobile bottom nav keeps Calendar / Bookings / Check In primary and places Members, Inventory, Reports, and Admin Guide in More. A floating ＋ FAB exposes Add Booking / Walk-in / Block Schedule.
  • Keyboard: Esc closes the topmost overlay; R refreshes data (suppressed while typing or when an overlay is open).
  • Live updates: Calendar and Bookings self-refresh via a realtime subscription on bookings (1.2 s debounce), a 60-second polling fallback, and an immediate refresh on tab re-focus.

6.2 Calendar

  • One-day timeline (9 AM–8 PM). Cards colored by assigned resource's color; status dot, ★ member badge, current-time line (today only); cancelled cards greyed/struck.
  • Date arrows, Today shortcut, month overlay with booking-dots; service tabs All (default) / Hotel / Grooming / Daycare / Studio with counts; resource filter via sidebar (desktop) or filter bottom-sheet (mobile).
  • Hotel stays render on every day of the stay (drop-off→end on check-in day, start→pick-up on check-out day, full-day between). Blocked schedules render as hatched blocks, column-laid-out beside bookings (never stacked), clipped to visible hours.

6.3 Bookings list & search

  • History list grouped by creation date (local timezone), newest first; collapsible groups; service filter chips.
  • Search (min 2 chars, debounced): reference number, owner first/last name, owner email, pet name — branch-scoped, replaces the list while active.
  • Pagination: 50-row pages via "Load earlier bookings" (loading state; definitive "You've reached the end" terminal state). Background refreshes preserve however many rows are expanded.

6.4 Booking drawer

  • Single source for one booking: ref + source + Print + ✏ Edit; pet header; status pills; creator/admin attribution when present; medical-notes alert; vaccines ✓/✕ with uploaded documents; grooming reference photos; payment receipt screenshots; waivers with operational warnings ("no media consent — do not post", "no playpark consent — keep in room"); service details; room/groomer assignment (required before check-in; "Assign" inline editor); owner card with call link; emergency + vet (hotel); itemized Bill (from booking charges, incl. weekday/weekend split, discount, convenience fee); Payments list + + Record; admin notes; check-in notes; sticky footer with status + payment-status selects and Update.
  • Record Payment: amount, type (downpayment / balance / add-on charge / refund), method (cash / card / bank transfer / online), reference #, notes, recorded-by. Add-on charge reveals a required add-on purpose selector (Dematting / Deshedding / Special instructions / Others), creates a booking add-on row, and adds the charge to the bill total. Amount must be a positive number.
  • Check-in notes per service — grooming: remarks + 5 yes/no condition checks (vaccination, skin&coat, ears/nose/eyes, nails, joints); hotel: physical inspection, personal items, add-on purchases; daycare: remarks + add-on purchases.

6.5 Creating & editing bookings (admin)

  • Add Booking wizard (Service → Schedule → Pet → Owner → Details → Summary): mirrors public pricing and data capture, but gives admins override power where operationally needed. Grooming uses an exact time input and may be saved even if a slot is already occupied; hotel includes an internal-only Other room for owner-provided cages. Owner email is optional for admin-created bookings. Owner step has a search-by-phone/email that reuses existing owner records; Summary applies member codes, sets initial status (pending/confirmed/checked-in) and payment status (auto-creating a payment record when paid), captures "recorded by", and sends the confirmation email immediately when enabled and an owner email is present.
  • Edit: opens the same wizard pre-filled at Schedule; service type is locked (cancel + rebook to change service); price-affecting edits recalculate totals and rewrite the itemized charges (preserving the online convenience fee); resource-affecting edits update the calendar instantly. Owner-detail edits detach from shared owner records when necessary so unrelated pets/bookings are not modified. An edit audit row is recorded.
  • Cancel: status → cancelled in the drawer; frees inventory, retains history. Refund: manual — record a Refund payment, set payment status refunded.

6.6 Check In page

  • Today's operational queue in three sections: Awaiting Check-In (due appointments + hotel arrivals, incl. overdue past dates), In Progress (checked-in), Due for Checkout (hotel checkouts today, with red ⚠ Overdue past their date). Cards sorted by time; same search box (searches all bookings); same drawer.

6.7 Members page

  • Validate in-store: code + pet name → ✓ valid (tier, coverage, expiry, registered pet, and breed when available) / pet-name mismatch / expired (with date) / not found-inactive.
  • CSV upload: system-of-record import with required headers Membership ID, Pet Name, Breed, Valid Until Date, Branch. The card provides a downloadable sample CSV, previews filename + row counts before import, lets the admin proceed/cancel, shows progress while processing, persists import status across page navigation/refresh, upserts duplicate membership IDs by code, and downloads a row-level report with status, result, and next action per row.

6.8 Inventory page

  • Tabs: Rooms / Groomers / Studios, branch-scoped, with counts. Create & edit: name + calendar color (8-color palette); rooms add cage type, pet type, allowed sizes; rooms support lock (+reason), groomers/studios support unavailable (+reason). Remove = soft delete: disappears from all pickers and public availability; historical bookings keep the name.
  • Selecting a groomer opens a scheduling drawer with a month calendar. Available days are marked; each date stores service start/end and a last-service cutoff. Admins can edit one day or bulk-overwrite multiple selected dates, and can view/add/edit/remove that groomer's blocked schedules.
  • A branch-scoped warning appears across the dashboard when any date from today through the next 13 days has no active groomer with assigned service hours.

6.9 Blocked schedules

  • Block a room / groomer / studio for one or more dates with a start–end time and reason. Removes availability from public + admin simultaneously; renders hatched on the calendar; groomer blocks can be edited from the Inventory resource drawer.

6.10 Reports

  • Grooming commission view filtered by date range and groomer. Includes completed grooming bookings only and summarizes subtotal sales, grooming service sales, all add-ons, and Dematting/Deshedding add-ons separately.
  • Report rows show date, pet name, selected grooming service, PHP subtotal, and a Demat/Deshed marker when applicable. Rows are clickable and open the same booking drawer used elsewhere.

Memberships

  • Record model: member code (e.g. BH-M001) tied to a registered pet name, optional pet breed, a tier, a validity date, an active flag, and (for Standard) a home branch.
  • Tiers: Standard valid at its home branch only · Passport valid at all branches.
  • Validation rules (same everywhere — public flow, admin wizard, Members page): code exists ∧ active ∧ not expired ∧ pet name matches ∧ branch in scope. Public-facing failures return a generic "Member not found." (no hint which check failed); the staff validator shows the precise reason.
  • Benefit: percentage discount on the booking subtotal — grooming 10%, hotel 20%, daycare 20% (rates DB-driven). Discounted bookings carry a ★ across admin surfaces and the code used is stored on the booking.
  • Administration: CSV bulk upload is the system of record (§6.7) — no per-member edit UI by design.

Pricing & business rules

All rates live in a single pricing database table and are fetched at runtime by both the public flow and the admin app — price changes require no deploy. If pricing fails to load, both surfaces show a persistent error banner and hard-block booking creation (guard against ₱0 bookings).

9.1 Price computation

ServiceFormula
Groomingpackage[size] + Σ add-ons (face trim by size; free with Premium; assessment items excluded from prepaid total). Ala Carte = add-ons only. Dematting/Deshedding affect duration immediately and may later be charged as admin add-on payments.
HotelΣ per night: rate[cage-type][weekday|weekend]. Cage type → rate key: small/medium/large cage → small/medium/large-dog rate; cat cabin / cat villa → own rates. Fri, Sat, Sun nights bill at the weekend rate. + late pick-up (below).
Daycarebase[size] (covers first 3 h) + extra[size] × max(0, hours − 3). Open-time = base now, extras at pickup.
StudioSettled at the studio.

9.2 Fees & discounts

ItemRule
Late pick-up (hotel)Per-hour fee for every hour past 2 PM; > 6 h past = +1 night; not collected by 8 PM = +1 night.
Member discount% of subtotal (base + add-ons + late fee): grooming 10 / hotel 20 / daycare 20.
Convenience feeOnline card/e-wallet checkout only; never on admin or walk-in bookings; excluded from discount base.
Itemized chargesEvery booking stores line items (base service or weekday/weekend night lines, late fee, discount, convenience fee, and admin-recorded add-on charges) so the admin bill always reconciles with the stored totals.

9.3 Operating hours (scheduling windows)

BranchMon–ThuFri–Sun
Estancia11:00 AM – 9:00 PM10:00 AM – 10:00 PM
Eastwood10:00 AM – 10:00 PM daily

9.4 Data conventions (functionally significant)

  • booking_date on a booking = creation date; the appointment date lives in each service's detail record (service_date, or check-in/out for hotel). All availability and check-in queues key off the latter.
  • Drop-off/pick-up hours stored as bare hour integers ("14"); grooming/studio slots as display strings ("2:00 PM").
  • Soft deletes everywhere user-visible (resources active=false, blocks active=false, bookings → cancelled) — nothing operational is hard-deleted.

Cost-saving optimizations & backend design initiatives

Operational safeguards that control Supabase usage, reduce unnecessary data transfer, and keep booking and payment writes reliable. “Implemented” describes the current codebase; “Recommended” identifies the next practical cost-control work.

11.1 Implemented cost controls

StatusInitiativeDesign and impact
ImplementedHomepage Storage egress reductionPublic galleries and blog covers use files deployed with the website instead of listing and downloading the same assets from Supabase Storage on every visit. This removes the landing page's largest recurring source of Supabase cached egress. The blog text remains database-driven.
ImplementedScoped admin queriesOperational views filter by branch, service, status, and date where applicable; list pages use explicit selects and booking pagination. This avoids repeatedly transferring the full booking dataset and related records to every admin screen.
ImplementedOn-demand private mediaVaccine files, grooming references, and payment receipts remain in a private bucket. The admin requests short-lived signed read URLs only when a booking drawer needs those files, rather than loading all media with booking lists.
ImplementedDirect signed uploadsThe upload Edge Function validates the request and issues a five-minute signed upload URL; the browser then sends the file directly to Storage. Large file bodies do not pass through the booking Edge Function, reducing function bandwidth and execution work.
ImplementedMinimal mutation responsesAdmin inserts, updates, upserts, and deletes normally request return=minimal, preventing Supabase from returning full rows when the UI does not need them.
ImplementedCached admin auth tokenThe admin client keeps the current access token in memory and refreshes it from auth-state events instead of calling getSession() before every REST request. This removes redundant auth work and prevents requests from stalling behind token refresh.
Cached egress is a metered usage counter, not a removable cache allocation. Moving public assets off Supabase stops those assets from adding future usage, but usage already recorded in the current billing cycle cannot be manually cleared; it resets with the plan's usage cycle.

11.2 Backend architecture and reliability

StatusInitiativeDesign and impact
ImplementedBrowser RLS + privileged Edge FunctionsPublic/admin browser access uses the anon key and database row-level security. Service-role credentials stay inside Edge Functions for controlled operations such as booking submission, payment completion, signed uploads, and pending-booking cancellation.
ImplementedIdempotent payment finalizationThe payment webhook finds the pending payload and atomically claims a pending booking before creating final child records. Duplicate webhook deliveries therefore do not create duplicate confirmed bookings or payments.
ImplementedPending inventory cleanupUnpaid bookings hold inventory temporarily, expire through scheduled cleanup, and can be cancelled immediately when a customer abandons or restarts checkout. This prevents stale reservations from consuming capacity.
ImplementedFailure cleanupBooking/payment functions remove partially created parent and detail rows when a downstream operation fails, limiting orphaned records and manual reconciliation.
ImplementedNormalized operational recordsCore bookings are separated from service details, itemized charges, add-ons, payments, uploads, waivers, owners, pets, and resources. Pricing and resources are database-driven, so routine rate and inventory changes do not require a website deployment.
ImplementedAuditability and soft deletionAdmin-created bookings, edits, and payments record the logged-in user. Operational records are cancelled or marked inactive instead of being destructively deleted, preserving history for support and reporting.

11.3 Recommended next optimizations

StatusInitiativeExpected benefit
RecommendedImage resizing and compressionCreate web-sized variants for uploaded photos and show thumbnails in admin lists/drawers, reserving originals for explicit viewing. This is the strongest remaining lever for Storage egress as upload volume grows.
RecommendedStorage retention policyDefine how long abandoned upload folders, superseded vaccine files, and old reference images must be retained, then schedule deletion for data that has no booking or has exceeded the business retention period.
RecommendedUsage monitoring by surfaceTrack Storage egress, database egress, function invocations, and largest objects by week. Add thresholds so unexpected growth is investigated before the plan limit is reached.
RecommendedQuery and index reviewUse Supabase query performance reports to add or verify indexes for the most common branch/date/status, owner lookup, member-code, and service-detail filters as production data grows.
RecommendedServer-side report aggregationIf reports become large, replace broad booking/detail downloads with a database view or RPC that returns only grouped totals and the requested rows for the selected groomer/date range.

Known gaps / out of scope

Editing a blocked schedule in place (delete & recreate is the accepted flow).
